Research

Research Interests

The group's research interests broadly lie at the intersection of machine learning and traditional statistical methods. This includes Bayesian Computational Methods, Bayesian Deep Learning, Machine Learning, Smoothing, Regularization and Shrinkage, Distributional Regression, Network Analysis as well as Spatial Statistics.

Ongoing Third-Party Research Projects

Prof. Klein is principal investigator in the following research projects.

  • from 2023: DFG Research Unit Fusing Deep Learning and Statistics towards Understanding Structured Biomedical Data (sub-project P5, sub-project P6)
  • since 2021: Experiment (funded by the Volkswagenstiftung)
  • since 2020: DFG individual grant (jointly with Andreas Mayr, University of Bonn)
  • since 2019: Emmy Noether Research Group (funded by the DFG)
